Common Side Effects

  • Bleeding (minor bruising to severe hemorrhage)
  • Gastrointestinal upset (nausea, dyspepsia, bleeding)
  • Allergic reactions (rash, itching)
  • Thrombocytopenia (low platelets, rare but serious)
  • Liver enzyme elevation (monitor liver function tests)

Serious Risks & Contraindications

  • Active bleeding or hemorrhagic disorders
  • Severe liver or kidney impairment (dose adjustments or avoidance needed)
  • Pregnancy and breastfeeding (warfarin contraindicated in pregnancy)
  • Recent surgery or trauma increasing bleeding risk
  • Hypersensitivity to any drug component

Mechanism of Action

Layman Explanation:

Anticoagulants and antiplatelets help prevent harmful blood clots by either slowing down the body's clotting proteins or by stopping platelets (small blood cells) from sticking together. This keeps blood flowing smoothly and prevents blockages.

Scientific Explanation:

Vitamin K antagonists (Warfarin): Inhibit vitamin K epoxide reductase complex 1, preventing recycling of vitamin K and reducing activation of clotting factors II, VII, IX, and X.
Direct Oral Anticoagulants (DOACs): Include direct thrombin inhibitors (dabigatran) and direct factor Xa inhibitors (apixaban, rivaroxaban, edoxaban). They inhibit key enzymes in the coagulation cascade, preventing fibrin clot formation.
Antiplatelets: Aspirin irreversibly inhibits cyclooxygenase-1 (COX-1), blocking thromboxane A2 synthesis and platelet aggregation. Clopidogrel and ticagrelor block P2Y12 ADP receptors on platelets, reducing platelet activation.
Pharmacokinetics: Warfarin is highly protein bound (>95%) with a long half-life (~40h); DOACs have shorter half-lives (5-17h), variable renal clearance. Antiplatelet effects vary by drug and irreversible/reversible binding.
