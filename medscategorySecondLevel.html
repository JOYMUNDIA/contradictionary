Common Side Effects

  • Bleeding (nosebleeds, gum bleeding, heavy menstruation, GI bleeding)
  • Bruising easily
  • Fatigue, dizziness (due to blood loss)
  • GI upset (dabigatran-specific)

Serious Risks & Contraindications

  • Active bleeding or high bleeding risk
  • Recent major surgery or trauma
  • Severe liver disease
  • Pregnancy (especially warfarin)
  • Mechanical heart valves (some DOACs not approved)

Mechanism of Action

Layman Explanation:

Anticoagulants prevent harmful blood clots by interrupting the body‚Äôs clotting process. They don‚Äôt dissolve existing clots but help stop new ones from forming.

Scientific Explanation:

Warfarin: Inhibits vitamin K epoxide reductase, reducing synthesis of clotting factors II, VII, IX, and X.
DOACs (Apixaban, Rivaroxaban, Edoxaban): Directly inhibit Factor Xa, preventing thrombin generation.
Dabigatran: Directly inhibits thrombin (Factor IIa), blocking the conversion of fibrinogen to fibrin.

Drug Classes:

  • Vitamin K Antagonists (VKAs): Warfarin
  • Direct Oral Anticoagulants (DOACs):
    • Factor Xa Inhibitors: Apixaban, Rivaroxaban, Edoxaban
    • Thrombin Inhibitor: Dabigatran

Pharmacokinetics Summary:

  • Warfarin: Oral bioavailability ~100%; highly protein-bound (>95%); metabolized by CYP2C9; half-life ~36‚Äì42 hours; narrow therapeutic index; monitored by INR.
  • Apixaban: Oral; half-life ~12 hours; metabolized by CYP3A4 and P-gp substrate; renal & fecal excretion.
  • Rivaroxaban: Oral; half-life ~5‚Äì9 hrs (young), 11‚Äì13 hrs (elderly); taken with food; hepatic and renal clearance.
  • Edoxaban: Oral; half-life ~10‚Äì14 hours; minimal CYP metabolism; ~50% renal clearance.
  • Dabigatran: Prodrug; hydrolyzed after oral absorption; half-life ~12‚Äì17 hours; ~80% renal excretion; requires acidic environment for absorption.

Clinical Considerations:

  • Monitoring: Warfarin requires INR checks; DOACs generally do not.
  • Reversal Agents: Vitamin K (warfarin), Andexanet alfa (Factor Xa inhibitors), Idarucizumab (dabigatran).
  • Dosing Frequency: Most DOACs are once or twice daily; warfarin dosing individualized based on INR.

Note: Anticoagulant choice depends on clinical scenario (e.g., atrial fibrillation, VTE), renal function, patient age, bleeding risk, and drug interactions.
