üß™ Scientific Background

CYP3A4 is a key enzyme for drug metabolism in the gut and liver.

Furanocoumarins in grapefruit irreversibly inhibit CYP3A4.

This leads to reduced metabolism, increased drug levels, and potential toxicity.

Effect lasts up to 72 hours after consumption.

üî¨ What is CYP3A4?

CYP3A4 is an enzyme that helps your body break down medications in the small intestine and liver.

Grapefruit inhibits this enzyme, causing drugs to build up in your system and possibly leading to side effects or overdose.

This inhibition is irreversible until new enzyme is produced, which can take up to 3 days.
